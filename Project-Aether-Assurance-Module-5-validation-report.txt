CANONICAL NORMALIZATION BOUNDARY
- sites own stable site/candidate identity, project/pathway relationship, active-context locator, context revision, owner, evidence references, and limitations.
- applicabilityContexts own versioned country/state/county/city/utility/watershed/tribal-context metadata, evidence, verification, revision, supersession, and limitations. They do not assert geospatial or legal facts.
- standards own original corporate, legal, permit, lender, insurer, utility, contract, tribal, community, project, voluntary, public-claim, and research sources with scope, unit, operator, evidence, dates, revision, exceptions, owner, verifier, and limitations.
- standardApplications own site/project/context-specific applicability and precedence decisions. They reference original standards and do not overwrite or duplicate source requirements.
- candidates own comparison identity and canonical source references. Eligibility is derived rather than stored as a replacement decision.
- comparisonSets own candidate membership, declared comparison scope, unit/scope warnings, owner, reviewer, and limitations.
- portfolios own project/candidate/comparison membership, governance, resource/concentration metadata, and limitations. Portfolio readiness is derived.
- escalations remains the sole escalation collection.
- Existing assurance records, permits, commitments, studies, milestones, dependency links, construction controls, inspections, construction reports, findings, CAPA actions, stop-work directives, decisions, evidence references, and local history retain their prior ownership.

PRECEDENCE, CONTEXT, COMPARISON, AND PORTFOLIO RESULT
- Verified comparable sources use the stricter verified requirement.
- Unverified local research remains a research gap and does not become binding law.
- A corporate minimum cannot weaken a verified legal, permit, contract, commitment, utility, lender, insurer, tribal, community, stop-work, or failed-hard-gate control.
- Incomparable scope, unit, or operator produces a manual-review result rather than false numeric precedence.
- Expired or superseded evidence reopens the standard/applicability conclusion.
- Changing a site's active context invalidates prior location-dependent standard applications and reopens permit applicability and agency-path assumptions until reverified.
- Missing evidence remains missing and does not improve comparison eligibility or score.
- Failed hard gates and active stop-work controls are evaluated before optimization.
- Open critical CAPA and unverified containment remain visible in comparison and portfolio output.
- Repeated cross-project themes reuse the canonical escalations collection without replacing source findings.
- Portfolio aggregation preserves project, land-close, milestone, construction/stop-work, closeout, comparison, and portfolio outputs as separate layers.
